Application of the Kyungu Formula to the Inverse Laplace Transform of \( \arctan\left(\frac{1}{p^2}\right) \)

Author: Pathy Kyungu
Publication Date: July 20, 2025
Version: 1.0 — Official Published Version

Abstract

This work presents the application of the Kyungu Formula to the inverse Laplace transform of the function: \[ F(p) = \arctan\left( \frac{1}{p^2} \right) \] A power series representation is obtained, followed by a simplification into a closed-form expression involving hyperbolic and trigonometric functions.

1. Kyungu Formula

The Kyungu Formula for the inverse Laplace transform is: \[ \mathcal{L}^{-1}[F](t) = \varphi(0) \cdot \delta(t) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{\varphi^{(n)}(0)}{(n!)^2} \cdot n \cdot t^{n-1} \] where: \[ \varphi(p) = F\left( \frac{1}{p} \right) \] and \( \delta(t) \) is the Dirac delta function.

2. Application to \( F(p) = \arctan\left( \frac{1}{p^2} \right) \)

2.1 Auxiliary Function

Given: \[ F(p) = \arctan\left( \frac{1}{p^2} \right) \] the auxiliary function becomes: \[ \varphi(p) = \arctan(p^2) \]

2.2 Series Expansion

Using: \[ \arctan(x) = \sum_{k=0}^\infty (-1)^k \cdot \frac{x^{2k+1}}{2k+1} \] we find: \[ \varphi(p) = \sum_{k=0}^\infty (-1)^k \cdot \frac{p^{4k+2}}{2k+1} \]

2.3 Derivatives

The derivatives at zero are: \[ \varphi^{(4k+2)}(0) = (-1)^k \cdot \frac{(4k+2)!}{2k+1} \]

2.4 Inverse Laplace Transform

Therefore: \[ \mathcal{L}^{-1}[F](t) = 2 \sum_{k=0}^\infty (-1)^k \cdot \frac{t^{4k+1}}{(4k+2)!} \]

3. Closed-form Expression

The series can be summed into the closed-form: \[ S(t) = \frac{2}{t} \cdot \sinh\left( \frac{t \sqrt{2}}{2} \right) \cdot \sin\left( \frac{t \sqrt{2}}{2} \right) \]

4. Conclusion

Thus, the final result is: \[ \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left[ \arctan\left( \frac{1}{p^2} \right) \right](t) = \frac{2}{t} \cdot \sinh\left( \frac{t \sqrt{2}}{2} \right) \cdot \sin\left( \frac{t \sqrt{2}}{2} \right) \]

This expression does not appear in standard Laplace transform tables. It highlights the analytical power of the Kyungu Formula.
