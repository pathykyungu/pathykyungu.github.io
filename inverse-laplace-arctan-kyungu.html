Application of the Kyungu Formula to the Inverse Laplace Transform of \\(\arctan\left(\frac{1}{p^2}\right)\\)

Author: Pathy Kyungu

Publication Date: July 20, 2025

Version: 1.0 — Official Published Version

Abstract

This article presents the application of the Kyungu Formula to the inverse Laplace transform of the function \\( \arctan\left( \frac{1}{p^2} \right) \\). A power series expression is obtained and then simplified into a closed-form expression involving hyperbolic and trigonometric functions. This original result highlights the efficiency of the Kyungu Formula in analytically computing complex inverse Laplace transforms.

1. Kyungu Formula

The Kyungu Formula for the inverse Laplace transform of a function \\( F(p) \\) is given by:

\\[ \mathcal{L}^{-1}[F](t) = \varphi(0) \cdot \delta(t) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{\varphi^{(n)}(0)}{(n!)^2} \cdot n \cdot t^{n-1} \\]

Where \\( \varphi(p) = F\left( \frac{1}{p} \right) \\) and \\( \delta(t) \\) is the Dirac delta distribution.

2. Application to \\( F(p) = \arctan\left( \frac{1}{p^2} \right) \\)

2.1 Auxiliary Function

\\[ F(p) = \arctan\left( \frac{1}{p^2} \right) \\]

\\[ \varphi(p) = F\left( \frac{1}{p} \right) = \arctan(p^2) \\]

2.2 Series Expansion

Using the classical expansion:

\\[ \arctan(x) = \sum_{k=0}^\infty (-1)^k \cdot \frac{x^{2k+1}}{2k+1} \\]

We get:

\\[ \varphi(p) = \arctan(p^2) = \sum_{k=0}^\infty (-1)^k \cdot \frac{p^{4k+2}}{2k+1} \\]

2.3 Derivatives

\\[ \varphi^{(4k+2)}(0) = (-1)^k \cdot \frac{(4k+2)!}{2k+1} \\]

2.4 Inverse Laplace Transform

Using the Kyungu Formula:

\\[ \mathcal{L}^{-1}[F](t) = 2 \sum_{k=0}^\infty (-1)^k \cdot \frac{t^{4k+1}}{(4k+2)!} \\]

3. Closed-form Expression

The resulting series:

\\[ S(t) = 2 \sum_{k=0}^\infty (-1)^k \cdot \frac{t^{4k+1}}{(4k+2)!} \\]

Has the following closed-form:

\\[ S(t) = \frac{2}{t} \cdot \sinh\left( \frac{t \sqrt{2}}{2} \right) \cdot \sin\left( \frac{t \sqrt{2}}{2} \right) \\]

4. Conclusion

We conclude that:

\\[ \mathcal{L}^{-1}\left[ \arctan\left( \frac{1}{p^2} \right) \right](t) = \frac{2}{t} \cdot \sinh\left( \frac{t \sqrt{2}}{2} \right) \cdot \sin\left( \frac{t \sqrt{2}}{2} \right) \\]

This result is not present in classical Laplace transform tables. It demonstrates the effectiveness of the Kyungu Formula in analytically deriving complex inverse Laplace transforms.
